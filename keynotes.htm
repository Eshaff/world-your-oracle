Biography

My work is rooted in ritual, story and song as well as in feminist thealogy and women's studies scholarship. As a Ph.D., I honed my skills in the emerging field of Women's Studies in the 1970s and 1980s, evolving an empowering leadership and teaching style that I use in my retreats and workshops today. I believe that my ongoing research in myth is accessible and inviting. (And that my workshops are playful and fun!)

I've offered ecofeminist classes, workshops and keynotes since 1987. In 1992, I left the Women's Studies Program at the University of Wisconsin-Madison to work full-time as a singer, storyteller, educator, workshop facilitator and ritualist. Since then I've presented workshops and sung all over North America. My approach is both creative and intellectual, using the skills that created my recording Chants for the Queen of Heaven as well as those that led to my Ph.D.

My efforts recently received recognition in the form of a Feminist Theology Award from the Unitarian Universlaist Women's Federation.

I live in Madison, WI, with a feminist spouse. My self-empowered, teenage daughter just flew the coop.
